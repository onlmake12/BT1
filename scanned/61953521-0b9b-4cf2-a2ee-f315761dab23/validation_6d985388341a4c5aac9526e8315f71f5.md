### Title
Multiple Sequential Integer Divisions in `dao_field_with_current_epoch` Cause Compounded Rounding Errors in DAO Secondary Issuance Accounting - (File: util/dao/src/lib.rs)

### Summary
In `dao_field_with_current_epoch`, two independent integer divisions both divide by `parent_c` (total circulating capacity). Each division independently truncates (floors) its result, introducing up to 1 shannon of rounding error per calculation per block. The errors compound over time: miners systematically receive slightly less secondary reward, and DAO depositors accumulate a slightly understated `ar` (accumulation rate), causing them to receive slightly less interest on withdrawal.

### Finding Description
In `util/dao/src/lib.rs`, `dao_field_with_current_epoch` performs two separate integer divisions sharing the same denominator `parent_c`:

**Division 1 — miner secondary issuance** (lines 242–243):
```rust
let miner_issuance128 = u128::from(current_g2.as_u64()) * u128::from(parent_u.as_u64())
    / u128::from(parent_c.as_u64());
``` [1](#0-0) 

**Division 2 — accumulation rate increase** (lines 256–257):
```rust
let ar_increase128 =
    u128::from(parent_ar) * u128::from(current_g2.as_u64()) / u128::from(parent_c.as_u64());
``` [2](#0-1) 

Both divisions truncate independently. The `nervosdao_issuance` is then derived as the complement:
```rust
let nervosdao_issuance = current_g2.safe_sub(miner_issuance)?;
``` [3](#0-2) 

Because `miner_issuance = floor(current_g2 * parent_u / parent_c)`, the truncated remainder goes to NervosDAO rather than the miner. Simultaneously, `ar_increase = floor(parent_ar * current_g2 / parent_c)` causes `current_ar` to grow slightly slower than the exact value each block.

The understated `current_ar` then compounds into DAO withdrawal calculations in `calculate_maximum_withdraw`:
```rust
let withdraw_counted_capacity = u128::from(counted_capacity.as_u64())
    * u128::from(withdrawing_ar)
    / u128::from(deposit_ar);
``` [4](#0-3) 

Since `withdrawing_ar` is the accumulated `current_ar` from many blocks, each of which was truncated by up to 1 unit, the withdrawal amount is slightly less than the exact entitlement.

### Impact Explanation
- **Miners**: Lose up to 1 shannon per block in secondary reward. Over 1 million blocks, this is up to ~1 million shannons (~0.01 CKB) per miner, systematically redirected to NervosDAO.
- **DAO depositors**: The accumulated understatement of `ar` over N blocks is at most N units (out of a base of 10^16). For a 1-billion-CKB deposit over 1 million blocks, the withdrawal shortfall is approximately `10^17 * 10^6 / 10^16 = 10^7 shannons ≈ 0.1 CKB`. For large, long-term deposits the compounding effect is measurable.
- The rounding direction is consistent: NervosDAO benefits at the expense of miners; DAO depositors are slightly disadvantaged on withdrawal.

### Likelihood Explanation
This occurs on every block unconditionally. Every call to `dao_field_with_current_epoch` (triggered by any block producer building a valid block) executes both divisions. The effect is deterministic and cumulative across the entire chain lifetime.

### Recommendation
Combine the two divisions to share a single truncation where possible, analogous to the River.sol fix. For `miner_issuance` and `ar_increase`, since both share the same denominator `parent_c`, the rounding errors could be reduced by computing them in a combined expression or by using a higher-precision intermediate before truncating once. At minimum, the rounding direction should be documented explicitly so that protocol designers can verify it matches the intended economic policy (i.e., that NervosDAO intentionally benefits from the truncation of miner secondary issuance).

### Proof of Concept
Let `current_g2 = 7`, `parent_u = 3`, `parent_c = 10`, `parent_ar = 10^16`:

- Exact `miner_issuance = 7 * 3 / 10 = 2.1` → truncated to `2` (miner loses 0.1 shannons)
- `nervosdao_issuance = 7 - 2 = 5` (exact would be `4.9`; NervosDAO gains 0.1 shannons)
- Exact `ar_increase = 10^16 * 7 / 10 = 7 * 10^15` → truncated to `7 * 10^15` (no error here in this example, but with non-round numbers the truncation applies)

With `current_g2 = 7`, `parent_c = 10`, `parent_ar = 10^16 + 3`:
- Exact `ar_increase = (10^16 + 3) * 7 / 10 = 7 * 10^15 + 2.1` → truncated to `7 * 10^15 + 2`
- Error: 0.1 units per block, accumulating over millions of blocks into a measurable withdrawal shortfall for large DAO deposits. [5](#0-4)
