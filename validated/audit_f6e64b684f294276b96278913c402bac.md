### Title
`Scheduler.sol` `FUTURE_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD` Too Small for L2 Chains with Timestamp Divergence — (`target_chains/ethereum/contracts/contracts/pulse/Scheduler.sol`)

### Summary

`Scheduler.updatePriceFeeds` passes `block.timestamp + FUTURE_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD` (only **10 seconds**) as `maxPublishTime` to `parsePriceFeedUpdatesWithConfig`. Pythnet's `publishTime` is derived from Pythnet's own clock (real-world time), while the target chain's `block.timestamp` is the L2 sequencer's clock. On L2 chains where `block.timestamp` lags real-world time by more than 10 seconds, every valid Pythnet price update is rejected with `PriceFeedNotFoundWithinRange`, permanently breaking the Scheduler for that chain.

### Finding Description

In `Scheduler.updatePriceFeeds`, the maximum acceptable publish time is computed as:

```solidity
uint64 curTime = SafeCast.toUint64(block.timestamp);
pyth.parsePriceFeedUpdatesWithConfig{value: pythFee}(
    updateData,
    params.priceIds,
    0,
    curTime + FUTURE_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD,  // block.timestamp + 10 seconds
    ...
);
``` [1](#0-0) 

`FUTURE_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD` is defined as only 10 seconds: [2](#0-1) 

Pythnet's `publishTime` is stamped by Pythnet's own clock, which tracks real-world UTC time. The target chain's `block.timestamp` is the L2 sequencer's clock. These two clocks are **not linked**. On L2 chains (Arbitrum, Blast, OP-stack chains), the sequencer's `block.timestamp` can legally lag real-world time. Arbitrum's documentation explicitly states the sequencer can set timestamps up to **24 hours earlier** than the current time. Empirically, Blast's `block.timestamp` was observed to be months behind Ethereum mainnet's timestamp during its early operation.

When `publishTime_pythnet > block.timestamp_L2 + 10s`, `parsePriceFeedUpdatesWithConfig` reverts with `PriceFeedNotFoundWithinRange`, and `updatePriceFeeds` fails entirely.

### Impact Explanation

- All calls to `Scheduler.updatePriceFeeds` revert on affected chains.
- Active subscriptions cannot receive price updates; `status.priceLastUpdatedAt` is never advanced.
- Protocols relying on the Scheduler for on-chain price feeds receive permanently stale data.
- Keepers cannot earn fees, removing economic incentive to retry.
- Subscription funds are locked (balance is decremented before the revert path is reached — see line 305 — but the revert rolls back the entire transaction, so funds are not lost; however the service is completely non-functional). [3](#0-2) 

### Likelihood Explanation

- The Scheduler is an EVM contract intended for deployment on any EVM-compatible chain, including L2s.
- Any L2 where the sequencer's `block.timestamp` lags real-world time by more than 10 seconds triggers the bug. This is a documented property of Arbitrum and OP-stack chains.
- No privileged access or attacker action is required — the condition arises from normal chain operation.
- The 10-second window is extremely tight; even minor sequencer clock drift causes failures.

### Recommendation

Increase `FUTURE_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD` to a value that accommodates realistic L2 timestamp divergence (e.g., 60–120 seconds), or decouple the future-timestamp guard from `parsePriceFeedUpdatesWithConfig` and enforce it separately in `_validateShouldUpdatePrices` using a more generous bound, similar to how `PAST_TIMESTAMP_MAX_VALIDITY_PERIOD` (1 hour) is already handled there. [4](#0-3) 

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy `Scheduler` on a chain where the sequencer's `block.timestamp` is 15 seconds behind real-world time (within documented Arbitrum/OP-stack bounds).
2. Pythnet publishes a price at real-world time `T`. The price update's `publishTime = T`.
3. A keeper calls `Scheduler.updatePriceFeeds(subscriptionId, updateData)`.
4. Inside `updatePriceFeeds`, `curTime = block.timestamp = T - 15`.
5. `maxPublishTime = curTime + 10 = T - 5`.
6. `parsePriceFeedUpdatesWithConfig` is called with `maxPublishTime = T - 5`, but the update has `publishTime = T > T - 5`.
7. The Pyth contract reverts with `PriceFeedNotFoundWithinRange`.
8. `updatePriceFeeds` reverts. No subscription can be updated. [5](#0-4)
