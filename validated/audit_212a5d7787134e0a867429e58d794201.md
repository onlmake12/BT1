### Title
Unweighted Arithmetic Mean in `FeeRateCollector::statistics` Skews Fee Rate Estimate Toward High-Fee Outliers — (`File: rpc/src/util/fee_rate.rs`)

---

### Summary

`FeeRateCollector::statistics` computes the `mean` field of `FeeRateStatistics` as a plain arithmetic mean of per-transaction fee rates, with no weighting by transaction weight. A single tiny transaction carrying an outsized fee rate contributes equally to the mean as a large, normal transaction. This mirrors the `TwapOracle.consult` flaw: large values dominate the aggregate, masking the true distribution and enabling a low-cost attacker to inflate the reported mean fee rate returned by the `get_fee_rate_statistics` RPC.

---

### Finding Description

`FeeRateCollector::statistics` iterates over the last `target` confirmed blocks, extracts each non-cellbase transaction's fee rate (`fee * 1000 / weight`), and pushes every result as a flat `u64` into a `Vec<u64>`. The `mean` is then computed as:

```rust
fn mean(numbers: &[u64]) -> u64 {
    let sum: u128 = numbers.iter().map(|number| u128::from(*number)).sum();
    (sum / numbers.len() as u128) as u64
}
``` [1](#0-0) 

Every transaction contributes exactly one sample regardless of its weight. A 100-byte transaction with a 1 000 000 shannons/kw fee rate contributes the same weight to the mean as a 100 000-byte transaction with a 1 000 shannons/kw fee rate.

The resulting `mean` is then surfaced directly in the RPC response:

```rust
Some(FeeRateStatistics {
    mean: mean(&fee_rates).into(),
    median: median(&mut fee_rates).into(),
})
``` [2](#0-1) 

This is exposed via both `get_fee_rate_statistics` and the deprecated `get_fee_rate_statics`: [3](#0-2) 

The individual per-transaction fee rate is computed as:

```rust
FeeRate::from_u64(fee.as_u64().saturating_mul(KW) / weight)
``` [4](#0-3) 

where `weight = max(tx_size_bytes, cycles * DEFAULT_BYTES_PER_CYCLES)`: [5](#0-4) 

Because the mean is unweighted, a single tiny transaction (e.g., 100 bytes, 0 cycles, fee = 10 000 shannons → fee rate = 100 000 shannons/kw) mixed with 99 normal transactions at 1 000 shannons/kw yields a mean of ≈ 1 990 shannons/kw — nearly double the true representative rate — even though the outlier represents a negligible fraction of total block weight.

---

### Impact Explanation

Any wallet or tooling that calls `get_fee_rate_statistics` and uses the `mean` field to set transaction fees will overpay when the mean is inflated. The `mean` is the only aggregated scalar value that is not robust to outliers; the `median` is also returned and is resistant to this manipulation, but the `mean` is explicitly documented and returned as a first-class field, making it a natural choice for fee estimation by downstream consumers.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires only that an adversary submit one or more transactions with an abnormally high fee rate that get confirmed. Because CKB's tx-pool admits any transaction meeting the minimum fee rate, and because miners are incentivized to include high-fee transactions, an attacker can reliably get such transactions mined at low absolute cost (a 100-byte transaction paying 10 000 shannons total is negligible). The `target` window defaults to 21 blocks and is capped at 101 blocks, so the manipulation persists for at most ~101 blocks before the outlier ages out. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

Replace the unweighted arithmetic mean with a **weight-weighted mean**: each transaction's fee rate should be weighted by its `weight` (bytes or cycle-equivalent), so that large transactions dominate the aggregate proportionally to their contribution to block space. Concretely:

```
weighted_mean = Σ(fee_i) * 1000 / Σ(weight_i)
```

This is equivalent to computing the aggregate fee rate of all transactions as a single pool, which is the natural and manipulation-resistant definition. Alternatively, document explicitly that the `mean` field is unweighted and that callers should prefer `median` for fee estimation.

---

### Proof of Concept

Assume `target = 21` blocks. In each of the last 21 blocks there are 100 normal transactions at 1 000 shannons/kw and 1 attacker transaction at 1 000 000 shannons/kw (100-byte tx, fee = 100 shannons).

- Total samples: 21 × 101 = 2 121
- Sum: 21 × (100 × 1 000 + 1 × 1 000 000) = 21 × 1 100 000 = 23 100 000
- Reported `mean`: 23 100 000 / 2 121 ≈ **10 891 shannons/kw** — nearly 11× the true representative rate of 1 000 shannons/kw

The attacker's cost per block: 1 × 100 shannons = negligible. A wallet using the `mean` would set fees ~11× higher than necessary, transferring value to miners. [7](#0-6)
