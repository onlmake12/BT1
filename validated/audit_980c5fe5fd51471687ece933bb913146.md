### Title
Compounding Floor-Division Truncation in NervosDAO Deposit/Withdraw Paired Operations Causes Depositors to Receive Less Interest Than Theoretical Maximum - (File: `util/dao/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

The NervosDAO interest calculation applies floor (truncating) integer division in two paired operations: `ar_increase` per block in `dao_field_with_current_epoch`, and `withdraw_counted_capacity` at withdrawal in `calculate_maximum_withdraw`. Both truncations reduce what depositors receive. Simultaneously, `nervosdao_issuance` is computed as a ceiling (because `miner_issuance` is floored), so the `s` accumulator grows faster than `ar` distributes interest. The difference is permanently stranded in `s` and never returned to depositors.

---

### Finding Description

**Operation 1 — `ar_increase` truncation (deposit-side, per block):**

In `dao_field_with_current_epoch`, the accumulate-rate increment is computed with floor division at every block:

```rust
// util/dao/src/lib.rs lines 256-258
let ar_increase128 =
    u128::from(parent_ar) * u128::from(current_g2.as_u64()) / u128::from(parent_c.as_u64());
let ar_increase = u64::try_from(ar_increase128).map_err(|_| DaoError::Overflow)?;
```

Because Rust integer division truncates toward zero, `ar_increase` loses up to 1 unit per block. Over N blocks, `ar` is underestimated by up to N shannons relative to the exact mathematical value.

**Operation 2 — `withdraw_counted_capacity` truncation (withdraw-side, per withdrawal):**

In `calculate_maximum_withdraw`, the interest-bearing portion of the withdrawal is also floored:

```rust
// util/dao/src/lib.rs lines 152-154
let withdraw_counted_capacity = u128::from(counted_capacity.as_u64())
    * u128::from(withdrawing_ar)
    / u128::from(deposit_ar);
```

This loses up to 1 additional shannon per withdrawal.

**The asymmetry that strands funds in `s`:**

In the same function, `miner_issuance` is floored, making `nervosdao_issuance` a ceiling:

```rust
// util/dao/src/lib.rs lines 242-246
let miner_issuance128 = u128::from(current_g2.as_u64()) * u128::from(parent_u.as_u64())
    / u128::from(parent_c.as_u64());
let miner_issuance = Capacity::shannons(...);
let nervosdao_issuance = current_g2.safe_sub(miner_issuance)?;
```

So `s` is credited with a ceiling value of `nervosdao_issuance` each block, but `ar` is incremented by a floor value. The `withdrawed_interests` subtracted from `s` is derived from `calculate_maximum_withdraw`, which itself uses the underestimated `ar`. The net result: `s` accumulates a persistent surplus that is never distributed to depositors.

```rust
// util/dao/src/lib.rs lines 252-254
let current_s = parent_s
    .safe_add(nervosdao_issuance)          // ceiling credit
    .and_then(|s| s.safe_sub(withdrawed_interests))?;  // floor debit
```

Both truncations act in the same direction — reducing what depositors receive — mirroring the allocate/remove asymmetry in the external report.

---

### Impact Explanation

Every NervosDAO depositor receives slightly less interest than the mathematically exact entitlement. The shortfall has two components:

- **Per-block `ar` drift**: at most 1 shannon per block. Over ~1.5 million blocks (≈1 year), `ar` is underestimated by up to ~1.5 million units. For a 1 billion CKByte (10¹⁷ shannon) deposit, the resulting interest loss is `10¹⁷ × 1.5×10⁶ / 10¹⁶ ≈ 15 shannons`.
- **Per-withdrawal floor**: at most 1 additional shannon per withdrawal.

Principal is never at risk (since `withdrawing_ar ≥ deposit_ar` is enforced by the block-number check at line 142). The stranded surplus accumulates in `s` indefinitely and is never returned to depositors. At scale — many depositors over many years — the aggregate stranded amount grows proportionally.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is triggered by any transaction sender who deposits into the NervosDAO and later withdraws. It occurs on every single DAO withdrawal on mainnet, unconditionally, with no special conditions required. The entry path is fully unprivileged: submit a deposit transaction, wait, submit a withdrawal transaction.

---

### Recommendation

- **Short term**: Document the rounding behavior explicitly for end users so they understand the theoretical maximum interest is not guaranteed to be distributed exactly.
- **Long term**: Consider using ceiling division for `ar_increase` (i.e., `(parent_ar * current_g2 + parent_c - 1) / parent_c`) to ensure `ar` grows at least as fast as the exact value, eliminating the systematic underestimation. Alternatively, track the rounding remainder and carry it forward to the next block.

---

### Proof of Concept

Alice, a NervosDAO depositor, deposits 1 billion CKBytes. After 1 year she initiates withdrawal.

1. Each block, `ar_increase` is floored: `ar` accumulates up to ~1.5 million units less than the exact value over 1 year.
2. At withdrawal, `withdraw_counted_capacity` is floored again.
3. Alice receives `counted_capacity × truncated_ar / deposit_ar + occupied_capacity` — up to ~16 shannons less than the exact mathematical entitlement.
4. The difference is permanently stranded in `s`.
