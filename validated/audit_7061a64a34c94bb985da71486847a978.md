### Title
Delegator Can Escape Slashing by Undelegating Before `slash` Is Applied to Their `DelegationRecord` - (File: `governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

In the Pyth Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) integrity pool, a delegator can avoid slashing by calling `undelegate` before the `slash` instruction is applied to their individual `DelegationRecord`. The `undelegate` instruction does not include `delegation_record` as an account and does not verify that all pending slash events have been processed, creating a race condition structurally identical to the LT contract vulnerability.

---

### Finding Description

The `DelegationRecord` account tracks two fields: `last_epoch` and `next_slash_event_index`. [1](#0-0) 

The `next_slash_event_index` field is the per-delegator pointer that tracks which `SlashEvent`s have been applied to that delegator's positions. Slashing is not applied atomically to all delegators at once. Instead, the `slash` instruction must be called individually for each delegator's `stake_account_positions` and `delegation_record`: [2](#0-1) 

This creates a window between when a `SlashEvent` is created on-chain and when `slash` is actually executed against each delegator's account.

The critical structural gap is that the `undelegate` instruction's account list does **not** include `delegation_record`: [3](#0-2) 

Because `undelegate` does not load or check `delegation_record`, it cannot enforce that `next_slash_event_index` is current (i.e., that all pending `SlashEvent`s have been applied). A delegator can therefore call `undelegate` to remove or transition their position out of the `LOCKED` state before `slash` is called on their account, leaving the `slash` instruction with a reduced or zero position to apply the ratio against.

Compare this to the `slash` instruction, which requires `delegation_record` as a writable account and modifies `stake_account_positions` based on the **current** position amount at execution time — not the historical amount at the epoch of the misprint: [4](#0-3) 

The `SlashEvent` stores `epoch` and `slash_ratio`, but the slash is applied to whatever position amount exists at the time `slash` is called, not the amount that was staked during the incident epoch. [5](#0-4) 

The slashing rulebook confirms the timeline gap: the Pythian Council has until the end of the epoch **following** the incident epoch to execute slashing — up to 14 days. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A delegator who monitors the chain for `SlashEvent` creation can call `undelegate` before `slash` is executed against their `delegation_record`. Their position is removed or placed into cooldown with a reduced amount, and when `slash` is eventually called on their account, it applies to a zero or reduced balance. The full slash burden is then redistributed among the remaining delegators who did not front-run. This directly undermines the OIS security model: the protocol is designed to penalize delegators who backed a misbehaving publisher, but the mechanism can be gamed by any delegator who acts within the 14-day window. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires no privileged access. Any delegator (`unstakeFromPublisher` is a public SDK function) can call `undelegate` at any time. [8](#0-7) 

`SlashEvent` creation is a public on-chain event. A delegator monitoring the chain for `SlashEvent` PDAs keyed to publishers they are delegated to has up to 14 days to react. The attack is straightforward to automate with a bot. Likelihood is **medium-high** for sophisticated delegators.

---

### Recommendation

The `undelegate` instruction should load the `delegation_record` for the relevant publisher and verify that `next_slash_event_index` equals the current total number of `SlashEvent`s for that publisher before allowing the position to be removed or transitioned. This mirrors the `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` guard pattern already present in the protocol: [9](#0-8) 

Alternatively, the `slash` instruction should apply the slash ratio retroactively based on the position amount recorded at the slash epoch, not the current position amount, so that undelegating after the incident epoch does not reduce the slashable amount.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**: Alice and Bob each delegate 500 PYTH to publisher P. Publisher P misbehaves in epoch E. The Pythian Council creates a `SlashEvent` for publisher P with `slash_ratio = 500 bps (5%)`.

**Attack Path**:

1. Alice monitors the chain and observes the `SlashEvent` PDA for publisher P being created on-chain.
2. Alice immediately calls `undelegate` (via `unstakeFromPublisher`) for her full 500 PYTH position with publisher P. Because `undelegate` does not load `delegation_record`, it succeeds without checking `next_slash_event_index`.
3. Alice's position is removed or placed into cooldown with 0 PYTH delegated to P.
4. The `reward_program_authority` calls `slash` for Alice's `delegation_record`. The instruction applies `slash_ratio` to Alice's current position amount of 0 PYTH → 0 PYTH slashed from Alice.
5. The `reward_program_authority` calls `slash` for Bob's `delegation_record`. Bob's 500 PYTH position is slashed at 5% → 25 PYTH slashed from Bob.
6. **Result**: Alice escapes her 25 PYTH slash. Bob bears the full pool-level slash burden. The DAO receives only 25 PYTH instead of the intended 50 PYTH. [10](#0-9)

```text
## Slashing

Slashing is an important aspect of the OIS protocol to ensure the integrity of the system.

The slashed amount for each pool is calculated as follows:

$$
\large{\bold{SL_p} = w \cdot S_p = w \cdot (S^{p}_{p} + S^{d}_{p})}
$$

Where:

- $SL_p$ is the slashed amount for the publisher $p$ pool.
- $w$ is the slashing rate.
- $S_p$ is the stake assigned to the publisher $p$ pool, made of self-staked amount $S^{p}_{p}$ and delegated stake $S^{d}_{p}$, or $S_{p} = S^{p}_{p} + S^{d}_{p}$.

Here $SL_p$ is uniformly allocated to both the self-staking publisher and delegators in the pool, pro-rata to their respective stake.

Subsequently, the net position of a publisher and delegators in a pool can be expressed as below:

$$
\large{\bold{\Pi^p_p} = S^p_p - w \cdot S^p_p}
$$

$$
\large{\bold{\Pi^d_p} = S^d_p - w \cdot S^d_p}
$$

Where:

- $\Pi^p_p$ is the net position of the publisher $p$ after slashing.
- $\Pi^d_p$ is the net position of the delegators after slashing.
- $w$ is the slashing rate.
- $S^p_p$ is the amount self-staked by the publisher $p$.
- $S^d_p$ is the amount delegated to the publisher $p$.
```

```typescript
  public async unstakeFromPublisher(
    stakeAccountPositions: PublicKey,
    publisher: PublicKey,
    positionState: PositionState.LOCKED | PositionState.LOCKING,
    amount: bigint,
  ) {
    const stakeAccountPositionsData = await this.getStakeAccountPositions(
      stakeAccountPositions,
    );
    const currentEpoch = await getCurrentEpoch(this.connection);

    let remainingAmount = amount;
    const instructionPromises: Promise<TransactionInstruction>[] = [];

    const eligiblePositions = stakeAccountPositionsData.data.positions
      .map((p, i) => ({ index: i, position: p }))
      .reverse()
      .filter(
        ({ position }) =>
          position.targetWithParameters.integrityPool?.publisher !==
            undefined &&
          position.targetWithParameters.integrityPool.publisher.equals(
            publisher,
          ) &&
          positionState === getPositionState(position, currentEpoch),
      );

    for (const { position, index } of eligiblePositions) {
      if (position.amount < remainingAmount) {
        instructionPromises.push(
          this.integrityPoolProgram.methods
            .undelegate(index, convertBigIntToBN(position.amount))
            .accounts({
              publisher,
              stakeAccountPositions,
            })
            .instruction(),
        );
        remainingAmount -= position.amount;
      } else {
        instructionPromises.push(
          this.integrityPoolProgram.methods
            .undelegate(index, convertBigIntToBN(remainingAmount))
            .accounts({
              publisher,
              stakeAccountPositions,
            })
            .instruction(),
        );
        break;
      }
    }

    const instructions = await Promise.all(instructionPromises);
    return sendTransaction(instructions, this.connection, this.wallet);
  }
```
