### Title
Pulse Keeper Network Fails to Update Price Feeds During Gas Price Spikes, Causing Stale Prices at Critical Market Moments - (File: `target_chains/ethereum/contracts/contracts/pulse/Scheduler.sol`)

---

### Summary

The Pyth Pulse (`Scheduler.sol`) keeper fee mechanism calculates reimbursement as `actualGasUsed * tx.gasprice`. During network congestion events — which historically coincide with market volatility — the computed keeper fee can exceed a subscription's on-chain balance, causing `updatePriceFeeds` to revert with `InsufficientBalance`. Keepers who pre-check balances off-chain will simply skip the update. The result is that Pulse-subscribed price feeds go stale at exactly the moment downstream DeFi protocols need accurate prices most.

---

### Finding Description

`_processFeesAndPayKeeper` in `Scheduler.sol` computes the total keeper reimbursement as:

```solidity
uint256 gasCost = (startGas - gasleft() + GAS_OVERHEAD) * tx.gasprice;
uint256 keeperSpecificFee = uint256(_state.singleUpdateKeeperFeeInWei) * numPriceIds;
uint256 totalKeeperFee = gasCost + keeperSpecificFee;

if (status.balanceInWei < totalKeeperFee) {
    revert SchedulerErrors.InsufficientBalance();
}
```

`gasCost` scales linearly with `tx.gasprice`. Subscription managers fund their subscriptions based on prevailing gas prices at deposit time. The governance-set `minimumBalancePerFeed` is also calibrated to normal gas conditions. During a gas price spike (10×–100× baseline), `gasCost` inflates proportionally, and the subscription balance — which was sufficient at deposit time — becomes insufficient to cover the keeper fee.

The check at line 295 only validates `balanceInWei >= pythFee` (the Pyth parsing fee), not `balanceInWei >= pythFee + totalKeeperFee`. The Pyth fee is deducted at line 305 before the keeper fee check at line 852. If the keeper fee check then fails, the entire transaction reverts (state is rolled back), but the keeper has already spent gas on the failed attempt. This further disincentivizes keepers from submitting during high-gas periods, since they bear the cost of reverted transactions.

The `GAS_OVERHEAD` constant of 30,000 gas units is a fixed estimate that does not adapt to network conditions.

---

### Impact Explanation

Pulse subscriptions are the push-oracle layer for DeFi protocols that cannot or do not integrate the pull model. When keeper updates fail:

1. `status.priceLastUpdatedAt` is not updated.
2. Calls to `getPricesNoOlderThan` revert with `StalePrice`, breaking any protocol that depends on freshness guarantees.
3. Calls to `getPricesUnsafe` return arbitrarily old prices, enabling mispriced liquidations, incorrect collateral valuations, and bad trade execution.

The severity is amplified by the correlation: gas prices spike during market volatility, which is precisely when accurate, fresh prices are most critical to downstream protocols.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This scenario is not hypothetical. The March 12, 2020 ETH crash (referenced in the external report) saw gas prices spike 10×–50× above baseline while ETH price fell 50% in hours. The same pattern repeated in May 2021 and during the LUNA collapse in May 2022. In each case, the protocols most dependent on accurate oracle prices were also the ones operating in the highest-gas environment. Subscription managers who funded their Pulse subscriptions at 10–20 gwei would find those subscriptions unable to be updated at 200–500 gwei, with no on-chain mechanism to top up in time.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Pre-flight balance check**: Add a combined check before deducting the Pyth fee — estimate the maximum possible keeper fee (e.g., using a gas price oracle or a configurable `maxGasPrice` parameter) and revert early if `balanceInWei < pythFee + estimatedMaxKeeperFee`. This avoids wasting keeper gas on doomed transactions.

2. **Cap keeper gas reimbursement**: Introduce a `maxGasPriceForReimbursement` governance parameter. Keepers are reimbursed at `min(tx.gasprice, maxGasPriceForReimbursement)`. This bounds the fee drain rate and allows subscription managers to size their deposits predictably.

3. **Dynamic minimum balance**: Tie `getMinimumBalance` to a gas price oracle so that the minimum deposit requirement reflects current network conditions, preventing underfunded subscriptions from being created or remaining active.

4. **Keeper incentive during congestion**: Consider a separate "emergency update" path that allows keepers to be paid a higher fixed premium (funded from a protocol reserve) when gas prices exceed a threshold, ensuring liveness during the exact conditions when it matters most.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Subscription manager calls `createSubscription` with `msg.value = 0.05 ETH` at 15 gwei gas price. The minimum balance check passes.
2. Gas price spikes to 500 gwei during a market crash.
3. A keeper calls `updatePriceFeeds(subscriptionId, updateData)`.
4. Inside `_processFeesAndPayKeeper`:
   - `gasUsed ≈ 200,000` (parsing + storage for a 5-feed subscription)
   - `gasCost = (200,000 + 30,000) * 500 gwei = 0.115 ETH`
   - `keeperSpecificFee = singleUpdateKeeperFeeInWei * 5` (small, e.g., 0.001 ETH)
   - `totalKeeperFee ≈ 0.116 ETH`
   - `status.balanceInWei = 0.05 ETH < 0.116 ETH` → `revert InsufficientBalance`
5. The keeper's transaction reverts; the keeper loses ~0.115 ETH in gas.
6. All keepers observe the insufficient balance off-chain and stop submitting.
7. `priceLastUpdatedAt` is never updated; `getPricesNoOlderThan` reverts for all callers.
8. DeFi protocols integrated with this Pulse subscription operate on stale prices during the crash. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3)

    function _processFeesAndPayKeeper(
        SchedulerStructs.SubscriptionStatus storage status,
        uint256 startGas,
        uint256 numPriceIds
    ) internal {
        // Calculate fee components
        uint256 gasCost = (startGas - gasleft() + GAS_OVERHEAD) * tx.gasprice;
        uint256 keeperSpecificFee = uint256(_state.singleUpdateKeeperFeeInWei) *
            numPriceIds;
        uint256 totalKeeperFee = gasCost + keeperSpecificFee;

        // Check balance
        if (status.balanceInWei < totalKeeperFee) {
            revert SchedulerErrors.InsufficientBalance();
        }

        status.balanceInWei -= totalKeeperFee;
        status.totalSpent += totalKeeperFee;

        // Pay keeper and update status
        (bool sent, ) = msg.sender.call{value: totalKeeperFee}("");
        if (!sent) {
            revert SchedulerErrors.KeeperPaymentFailed();
        }
    }
