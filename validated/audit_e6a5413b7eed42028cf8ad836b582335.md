### Title
OIS Delegators Can Avoid Slashing by Unstaking Before `slash` Is Applied to Their Account - (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

### Summary
The Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) slashing mechanism is split into two separate on-chain steps: `create_slash_event` (called by `rewardProgramAuthority`) and `slash` (permissionless, called per-staker). Because the Pythian Council investigation window spans up to two full epochs (~14 days) before `create_slash_event` is even submitted, and because `slash` must be called individually for each staker after the event is created, any staker can observe the pending slash and call `close_position` to move their stake to cooldown (`PREUNLOCKING`/`UNLOCKING`) before `slash` is executed against their account. If `slash_account` in the staking program only reduces positions in `LOCKED` state (the active staking state), the staker fully avoids the slash.

### Finding Description

The OIS slashing flow has two distinct on-chain phases:

**Phase 1 – `create_slash_event`**: Called by `rewardProgramAuthority`. Creates a `SlashEvent` PDA keyed by `(publisher, index)` storing `epoch`, `slash_ratio`, and `slash_custody`. [1](#0-0) 

**Phase 2 – `slash`**: Permissionless (any `signer`). Reads the `SlashEvent`, reads the staker's `DelegationRecord` (which tracks `next_slash_event_index` to prevent double-slashing), then CPI-calls `slash_account` in the staking program to reduce the staker's custody. [2](#0-1) 

The `DelegationRecord` per `(publisher, staker)` pair tracks `last_epoch` and `next_slash_event_index`: [3](#0-2) 

The staking program's `close_position` instruction is callable by the position owner at any time and transitions a position from `LOCKED` → `PREUNLOCKING` (cooldown epoch 1) → `UNLOCKING` (cooldown epoch 2) → `UNLOCKED`: [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4) 

The `slash_account` instruction in the staking program takes only `slash_ratio` and operates on current `stake_account_positions` — it does not take a historical epoch snapshot parameter: [6](#0-5) 

Because `slash_account` operates on current position state (not a historical snapshot), a staker who has moved to `PREUNLOCKING`/`UNLOCKING` before `slash` is called on their account will have no `LOCKED` positions for the publisher target, and the slash will reduce zero tokens from their stake.

### Impact Explanation

A staker who successfully frontruns the `slash` call avoids the entire slashing penalty. This directly undermines the OIS security model: the economic deterrent against delegating to misbehaving publishers is nullified for any staker who monitors the Pythian Council forum or the on-chain `create_slash_event` transaction. The slashed amount is reduced, meaning the DAO treasury receives less than intended and the publisher's pool is not fully penalized. Honest stakers who do not monitor and exit are disproportionately penalized relative to sophisticated stakers who do. [7](#0-6) 

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack window is exceptionally large. The Slashing Rulebook explicitly states the Pythian Council has until the end of the epoch following the incident epoch to conclude its investigation — up to ~14 days of public forum discussion before `create_slash_event` is even submitted: [8](#0-7) 

After `create_slash_event` is submitted, `slash` must be called individually for each of potentially thousands of stakers. Any staker monitoring the Pythian Council forum (public) or watching on-chain state can call `close_position` days or weeks before `slash` reaches their account. No special privileges, leaked keys, or Sybil attacks are required — only a standard user transaction. [9](#0-8) 

### Recommendation

1. **Snapshot-based slashing**: Record the staker's delegated amount at the `slash_event.epoch` (e.g., via the existing `DelegationRecord.last_epoch` and pool `events` array) and apply the slash ratio to that historical amount rather than the current `LOCKED` position. This ensures that unstaking after the incident epoch does not reduce the slashable amount.

2. **Slash cooldown positions**: Extend `slash_account` to also reduce positions in `PREUNLOCKING` and `UNLOCKING` states for the relevant publisher target, so that initiating cooldown after a misprint does not exempt a staker.

3. **Freeze unstaking on slash event creation**: When `create_slash_event` is called for a publisher, temporarily prevent `close_position` for positions delegated to that publisher until all `slash` calls for that event are processed. [3](#0-2) 

### Proof of Concept

1. Staker Alice delegates 1,000,000 PYTH to publisher P during epoch N.
2. Publisher P causes a data misprint during epoch N. The Pythian Council opens a public investigation on the forum.
3. Alice monitors the forum. During the investigation (epoch N or N+1), Alice calls `close_position` for her delegation to P. Her position transitions to `PREUNLOCKING` (cooldown).
4. At the end of epoch N+1, the Pythian Council calls `create_slash_event` with `slash_ratio = 500` (5%).
5. A relayer calls `slash(index)` for Alice's `stake_account_positions`. Alice's `delegation_record.next_slash_event_index` is updated, but her positions for publisher P are now in `PREUNLOCKING`/`UNLOCKING` state — not `LOCKED`. `slash_account` finds no `LOCKED` positions for publisher P and transfers 0 tokens to `slash_custody`.
6. Alice's 1,000,000 PYTH is fully preserved. She re-stakes after cooldown ends. [4](#0-3) [10](#0-9)

export const getPositionState = (
  position: Position,
  currentEpoch: bigint,
): PositionState => {
  if (currentEpoch < position.activationEpoch) {
    return PositionState.LOCKING;
  }
  if (!position.unlockingStart) {
    return PositionState.LOCKED;
  }
  const hasActivated = position.activationEpoch <= currentEpoch;
  const unlockStarted = position.unlockingStart <= currentEpoch;
  const unlockEnded = position.unlockingStart + 1n <= currentEpoch;

  if (hasActivated && !unlockStarted) {
    return PositionState.PREUNLOCKING;
  } else if (unlockStarted && !unlockEnded) {
    return PositionState.UNLOCKING;
  } else {
    return PositionState.UNLOCKED;
  }
};

### Slashing Calculation and Distribution

If slashing event confirmed, the Pythian Council will process calculation and distribution of the slashed stake according to the following:

- **Stake Slashed**
  - capped at 5% of the total amount staked (including the amount delegated) into pools associated with publishers identified as directly responsible for poor data quality. distribution of the slashed amount is uniform amongst publishers and delegator(s)
  - In the case the total amount staked by the stakers responsible for the data quality issue is nil, no slashing takes place
