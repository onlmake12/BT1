### Title
Unverified Publisher-to-StakeAccount Binding in `set_publisher_stake_account` Allows Publisher Self-Stake Inflation - (File: `governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

The `set_publisher_stake_account` instruction in the integrity pool program accepts a `new_stake_account_positions_option` account with no on-chain constraint verifying that the publisher is the owner of that stake account. A publisher can register any arbitrary stake account — including one belonging to a whale with a large PYTH balance — as their own publisher stake account. Because the publisher's self-stake is factored into reward calculations in `advance_delegation_record`, this inflates the publisher's reward share at the expense of honest delegators.

---

### Finding Description

The `set_publisher_stake_account` instruction accepts three key accounts:

```
signer          – must sign (checked at runtime: must be publisher or reward_authority)
publisher       – "CHECK: The publisher will be checked against data in the pool_data"
new_stake_account_positions_option  – optional, NO constraints
current_stake_account_positions_option – optional, NO constraints
```

The account constraints only ensure:
- `pool_config` is the correct PDA.
- `pool_data` is linked to `pool_config` via `relations`.
- `signer` is the publisher or reward authority (runtime check, evidenced by error `PublisherOrRewardAuthorityNeedsToSign`).

There is **no on-chain assertion** that the publisher is the owner of `new_stake_account_positions_option`. The staking program stores the owner of a `stake_account_positions` account in the first 32 bytes after the discriminator, but `set_publisher_stake_account` never reads or verifies this field against the `publisher` key.

Once registered, `pool_data` stores the publisher's stake account. In `advance_delegation_record`, the `publisher_stake_account_positions` (optional) is provided and its custody account is derived as a PDA from it. The publisher's self-stake balance in that custody account is used to compute the publisher's reward share. If the registered account belongs to a third party with a large PYTH balance, the publisher's apparent self-stake is inflated.

The `advance_delegation_record` IDL confirms `publisher_stake_account_positions` is accepted with no ownership constraint:

```
publisher_stake_account_positions  – optional (no PDA, no relation, no ownership check)
publisher_stake_account_custody    – optional PDA derived from publisher_stake_account_positions
``` [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A malicious publisher can:
1. Identify a whale's `stake_account_positions` with a large PYTH balance.
2. Call `set_publisher_stake_account` (signing as the publisher) with the whale's account as `new_stake_account_positions_option`.
3. `pool_data` now records the whale's stake account as the publisher's self-stake.
4. When `advance_delegation_record` is called, the publisher's self-stake appears inflated.
5. The publisher receives a disproportionately large share of pool rewards, draining rewards from honest delegators.

The reward pool is finite. Inflating one publisher's self-stake directly reduces the rewards available to all other participants. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The `set_publisher_stake_account` instruction is permissionless for any registered publisher (no governance or admin gating beyond the publisher signing).
- All stake account positions are public on-chain; a publisher can trivially enumerate large accounts.
- The attack requires only a single transaction from the publisher's key.
- No leaked keys, Sybil attacks, or privileged access are needed — only a registered publisher key. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Recommendation

Add an ownership check inside `set_publisher_stake_account` that reads the `owner` field from the `new_stake_account_positions_option` account data and asserts it equals the `publisher` key (or the `signer` key, which is verified to be the publisher). In Anchor terms, this can be expressed as a `constraint` or a `has_one` relation once the stake account positions account type is imported from the staking program.

```rust
// Pseudocode constraint to add:
require_keys_eq!(
    new_stake_account_positions.load_owner()?,
    publisher.key(),
    ErrorCode::PublisherStakeAccountMismatch
);
``` [5](#0-4) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Publisher `P` is registered in `pool_data`.
2. Whale `W` holds a `stake_account_positions` account with 10,000,000 PYTH.
3. `P` calls `set_publisher_stake_account` with:
   - `signer` = P's keypair
   - `publisher` = P's pubkey
   - `new_stake_account_positions_option` = W's `stake_account_positions` pubkey
4. `pool_data` now records W's stake account as P's publisher self-stake.
5. Any caller invokes `advance_delegation_record` for any delegator of P, passing W's stake account as `publisher_stake_account_positions`.
6. The reward calculation uses W's 10,000,000 PYTH as P's self-stake, granting P an inflated reward share.
7. P's `stake_account_custody` (their real one) receives excess rewards drained from the pool. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)

      .setPublisherStakeAccount()
      .accounts({

        newStakeAccountPositionsOption: newStakeAccountPositions ?? null,
