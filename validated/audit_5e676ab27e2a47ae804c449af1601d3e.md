### Title
Unprivileged `write_authority` Takeover in `init_encoded_vaa` Enables Rent Drain via `close_encoded_vaa` — (`target_chains/solana/programs/core-bridge/src/processor/parse_and_verify_vaa/init_encoded_vaa.rs`)

---

### Summary

`init_encoded_vaa` accepts any signer as `write_authority` with no binding to the account creator or payer. Because `close_encoded_vaa` transfers all lamports to whoever is stored as `write_authority`, an attacker who races to call `init_encoded_vaa` first on a freshly-created, uninitialized `encoded_vaa` account can permanently claim the victim's rent deposit.

---

### Finding Description

`InitEncodedVaa` enforces only two constraints on the `encoded_vaa` account: [1](#0-0) 

1. The account must be owned by `crate::ID`.
2. The header region must be all-zero bytes. [2](#0-1) 

`write_authority` is merely required to be a `Signer` — there is no constraint tying it to the account's creator or lamport funder: [3](#0-2) 

The function then unconditionally writes the caller-supplied `write_authority` into the account header: [4](#0-3) 

`close_encoded_vaa` later verifies only that the signer matches the stored `write_authority`, then transfers **all lamports** to that key: [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

The `write_authority` stored in `Header` is the sole authority checked: [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Any attacker who observes a confirmed `system_program::create_account` transaction (creating an account owned by the core-bridge program with a zeroed header) can immediately call `init_encoded_vaa` with their own keypair as `write_authority`. Once stored, they call `close_encoded_vaa` and receive all lamports the victim deposited for rent. The victim's account is closed and their SOL is gone. This is direct, unconditional theft of user funds in motion.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Solana has no traditional public mempool, but the attack does **not** require mempool access. It only requires monitoring confirmed on-chain state: once `create_account` is confirmed (slot N), the attacker submits `init_encoded_vaa` in slot N or N+1, before the victim's `init_encoded_vaa` lands. Any bot watching the chain for newly-created core-bridge-owned accounts with zeroed headers can reliably win this race. The attack is permissionless, requires no privileged role, and is repeatable against every relayer that separates account creation from initialization across transaction boundaries.

---

### Recommendation

Bind `write_authority` to the account creator at initialization time. The simplest fix is to require that `write_authority` also signs as the **payer** of the `encoded_vaa` account, or to derive `encoded_vaa` as a PDA seeded by `write_authority`. Concretely, add an `init` constraint (Anchor's `#[account(init, payer = write_authority, ...)]`) so the program itself creates and funds the account, making the payer and write authority identical by construction and eliminating the two-transaction window entirely.

---

### Proof of Concept

```rust
// Bankrun / program-test pseudocode
let victim   = Keypair::new();
let attacker = Keypair::new();
let vaa_kp   = Keypair::new();

// Step 1 – victim creates and funds the account
banks_client.process_transaction(Transaction::new_signed_with_payer(
    &[system_instruction::create_account(
        &victim.pubkey(), &vaa_kp.pubkey(),
        rent_lamports, account_size, &core_bridge::ID,
    )],
    Some(&victim.pubkey()), &[&victim, &vaa_kp], recent_blockhash,
)).await.unwrap();

// Step 2 – attacker front-runs init_encoded_vaa with their own write_authority
banks_client.process_transaction(Transaction::new_signed_with_payer(
    &[init_encoded_vaa_ix(
        write_authority: attacker.pubkey(),
        encoded_vaa:     vaa_kp.pubkey(),
    )],
    Some(&attacker.pubkey()), &[&attacker], recent_blockhash,
)).await.unwrap();

// Step 3 – attacker closes the account and receives victim's rent
let attacker_before = banks_client.get_balance(attacker.pubkey()).await.unwrap();
banks_client.process_transaction(Transaction::new_signed_with_payer(
    &[close_encoded_vaa_ix(
        write_authority: attacker.pubkey(),
        encoded_vaa:     vaa_kp.pubkey(),
    )],
    Some(&attacker.pubkey()), &[&attacker], recent_blockhash,
)).await.unwrap();
let attacker_after = banks_client.get_balance(attacker.pubkey()).await.unwrap();

// Assert attacker gained victim's rent deposit
assert!(attacker_after > attacker_before + rent_lamports - tx_fees);
```
