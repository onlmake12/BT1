### Title
OIS Slash Bypass via Cooldown-Window Race: Stakers Can Withdraw Before Slash Executes — (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`, `governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/utils/position.ts`)

---

### Summary

The Pyth Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) slashing process has a structural timing mismatch: the unstake cooldown period (1–2 epochs, i.e. 7–14 days) is not guaranteed to exceed the maximum slash execution window (up to 2 epochs, i.e. up to 14 days). A malicious publisher or delegator who observes a misprint incident can immediately call `undelegate`, wait out the cooldown, withdraw their tokens from the custody account, and thereby escape the slash entirely — because the `slash` instruction requires tokens to be physically present in `stakeAccountCustody` to transfer them to `slashCustody`. The staking program's `withdraw` path has no awareness of pending slash events in the integrity pool program.

---

### Finding Description

**Slashing timeline (from the Slashing Rulebook):**

> "The Pythian Council is responsible for analysing and delivering its conclusions within the same epoch when the potential slashing event happened or during the following epoch at the latest."

This means the slash can be executed as late as the end of epoch T+1 (up to ~14 days after the incident at epoch T). [1](#0-0) 

**Cooldown period:**

When a staker calls `undelegate`, their position transitions from `LOCKED` → `PREUNLOCKING` → `UNLOCKING` → `UNLOCKED`. The `UNLOCKED` state is reached when `unlockingStart + 1 <= currentEpoch`, meaning the position is fully unlocked after 1 full epoch of cooldown (7 days). The UI confirms tokens are available to withdraw "at the end of the next epoch." [2](#0-1) 

**The race window:**

| Event | Epoch |
|---|---|
| Misprint incident | T |
| Staker calls `undelegate` | T (immediately) |
| Position enters `PREUNLOCKING` | T |
| Position becomes `UNLOCKED` | T+2 |
| Staker withdraws tokens | T+2 |
| Pythian Council deadline | end of T+1 |

A staker who undelegates at the start of epoch T has their tokens fully unlocked by epoch T+2. The Pythian Council's deadline is end of epoch T+1. If the Council is slow (concludes near the end of T+1) and the staker withdraws promptly at T+2, the slash instruction finds an empty or reduced custody account.

**Cross-program isolation — the structural root cause:**

The `slash` instruction lives in the `integrity_pool` program and transfers tokens from `stakeAccountCustody` to `slashCustody`. [3](#0-2) 

The staking program's `withdraw` instruction has no knowledge of pending slash events in the integrity pool program. The staking IDL error set contains `InsufficientWithdrawableBalance` but no error for "pending slash event." [4](#0-3) 

Once tokens are withdrawn from `stakeAccountCustody`, the `slash` instruction cannot recover them — the transfer to `slashCustody` would fail or apply to a reduced balance.

**The `SlashEvent` epoch field does not prevent this:**

The `SlashEvent` struct stores the incident `epoch` and `slash_ratio`. [5](#0-4) 

Even if the program uses this epoch to identify the historically slashable amount, the physical tokens must still be present in the custody account for the slash transfer to succeed. Historical accounting does not prevent withdrawal.

---

### Impact Explanation

A malicious publisher or delegator who submits bad data (or observes they are about to be slashed) can:

1. Immediately call `undelegate` upon observing the misprint incident.
2. Wait 1–2 epochs for the cooldown to expire.
3. Withdraw all tokens from `stakeAccountCustody`.
4. The Pythian Council's subsequent `slash` call finds insufficient funds and cannot recover the slashable amount.

This directly undermines the economic security guarantee of OIS: publishers and delegators face no real slashing risk if they act quickly. The slashing rulebook states the slashable amount is "the tokens eligible for rewards **during the epoch of the misprint incident**," but the on-chain enforcement cannot recover tokens that have already been withdrawn. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- A publisher who submits bad data knows immediately when the incident occurs (they submitted it).
- The `undelegate` call is a standard, permissionless user action — no special access required.
- The cooldown window (1–2 epochs) overlaps with the slash execution window (up to 2 epochs).
- The attack requires only monitoring the chain and calling two instructions (`undelegate`, then `withdraw`), both of which are normal user operations.
- Delegators who observe a misprint incident on-chain can also front-run the slash.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Extend the cooldown period** to be strictly greater than the maximum slash execution window. If the Pythian Council has up to 2 epochs to conclude, the cooldown should be at least 3 epochs (21 days) to guarantee the slash can always be applied before tokens are withdrawable.

2. **Block withdrawals when pending slash events exist**: The staking program's `withdraw` instruction should cross-check the integrity pool program's `num_slash_events` for the relevant publisher(s) against the staker's `DelegationRecord.next_slash_event_index`. If there are unprocessed slash events, withdrawal should be blocked until the slash is applied. [7](#0-6) 

3. **Alternatively**, make the `slash` instruction apply based on the historical staked amount at the incident epoch (from the delegation record), and allow it to slash tokens even in `PREUNLOCKING`/`UNLOCKING`/`UNLOCKED` states, as long as the tokens remain in the custody account.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:** Publisher P stakes 1,000,000 PYTH in OIS at epoch T-1. At epoch T, P submits a bad price that causes a misprint incident.

**Attack:**
1. At the start of epoch T, P calls `undelegate(index, 1_000_000)` on the integrity pool program. Position transitions to `PREUNLOCKING` with `unlockingStart = T+1`. [8](#0-7) 

2. At epoch T+2, `unlockingStart + 1 <= currentEpoch` → position is `UNLOCKED`. [9](#0-8) 

3. P calls `withdraw(1_000_000)` on the staking program. Tokens leave `stakeAccountCustody`. No check for pending slash events occurs.

4. At end of epoch T+1, the Pythian Council calls `create_slash_event(index=0, slash_ratio=500)` (5% slash) and then `slash(index=0, ...)` targeting P's stake account.

5. The `slash` instruction attempts to transfer tokens from `stakeAccountCustody` to `slashCustody`, but the custody account is empty. The slash fails or applies to zero tokens.

**Result:** P escapes the full 50,000 PYTH slash (5% of 1,000,000 PYTH) by timing the unstake before the slash is executed. [10](#0-9)
