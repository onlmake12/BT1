### Title
Publisher Opt-Out of OIS Rewards Is Irreversible Without Governance Intervention — (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/pyth-staking-client.ts`, `governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

A publisher in the Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) program can unilaterally opt out of rewards by calling `removePublisherStakeAccount`, which sets their `publisherStakeAccounts` entry in `pool_data` to `None`. However, the `set_publisher_stake_account` on-chain instruction's authorization model requires the signer to be the owner of the **current** stake account. Once the current stake account is `None`, the publisher has no account to prove ownership of, and the only path to re-associate a stake account is through the governance multisig — a privileged, off-band process. The UI does not communicate this permanence, and the opt-out flow presents no warning that the action is irreversible without governance action.

---

### Finding Description

The `removePublisherStakeAccount` function in the SDK calls `setPublisherStakeAccount` with `newStakeAccountPositions = undefined` (i.e., `null`), effectively clearing the publisher's stake account entry:

```
removePublisherStakeAccount(publisher, stakeAccountPositions)
  → setPublisherStakeAccount(publisher, stakeAccountPositions, undefined)
    → currentStakeAccountPositionsOption = stakeAccountPositions
    → newStakeAccountPositionsOption     = null
``` [1](#0-0) 

The `set_publisher_stake_account` instruction's IDL shows that `signer` and `publisher` are **separate accounts** — the publisher is not required to sign: [2](#0-1) 

The authorization logic (inferred from the CLI tooling) requires the signer to be the owner of `currentStakeAccountPositionsOption`. When that field is `None` (post opt-out), there is no stake account to prove ownership of. The only supported path to set a stake account from `None` is through the governance multisig, which passes `INTEGRITY_POOL_PROGRAM_ID` as a placeholder for `None` and uses the vault authority as signer: [3](#0-2) 

The `pool_data` account stores `publisherStakeAccounts` as a fixed array of 1024 pubkeys. Once a publisher's entry is zeroed out, the publisher cannot self-service restore it. [4](#0-3) 

The UI opt-out modal warns only about losing rewards and explicitly states "You will still be able to participate in OIS after opting out of rewards" — with no mention that re-opting in requires governance action: [5](#0-4) 

There is no corresponding "opt back in" button or SDK method exposed to the publisher. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A publisher who opts out permanently loses:
- The **publisher yield rate** (self-staking rewards)
- **Delegation fees** from delegators staking to their pool

Without governance intervention (a multisig proposal, vote, and execution), the publisher cannot re-associate a stake account. This is a permanent financial loss for the publisher, not a temporary one. Delegators staking to that publisher's pool also lose the benefit of the publisher's self-stake (which affects pool cap and reward calculations).

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The opt-out UI explicitly says participation continues after opting out, creating a reasonable expectation that the action is reversible. Any publisher who opts out temporarily (e.g., to rotate a stake account, or during a maintenance window) and later attempts to opt back in will discover the action is irreversible without governance. The governance multisig process is slow and not self-service, making recovery impractical in practice.

---

### Recommendation

- **Short term:** Add a self-service opt-back-in path: allow the publisher (signing as themselves) to call `set_publisher_stake_account` with `currentStakeAccountPositionsOption = None` and a valid `newStakeAccountPositionsOption`, provided the signer is the publisher key registered in `pool_data.publishers`.
- **Long term:** Update the UI opt-out modal to clearly state that re-opting in requires governance action. Add unit/integration tests covering the full opt-out → opt-back-in state transition.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Publisher Alice calls `optPublisherOut` via the staking UI, which invokes `removePublisherStakeAccount(alicePublisherKey, aliceStakeAccount)`.
2. On-chain, `pool_data.publisherStakeAccounts[alice_index]` is set to `Pubkey::default()` (zeroed).
3. Alice later tries to opt back in by calling `setPublisherStakeAccount` with `currentStakeAccountPositionsOption = None` and `newStakeAccountPositionsOption = aliceStakeAccount`, signing with her wallet.
4. The on-chain program rejects the transaction because the signer is not the governance authority — the only authorized signer when `currentStakeAccountPositionsOption` is `None`.
5. Alice's stake account remains disassociated indefinitely. She earns no self-staking rewards and cannot recover without a governance multisig proposal.
