### Title
Staker Can Front-Run `slash` Execution by Undelegating After `create_slash_event` — (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

The Pyth Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) slashing workflow is a two-step process: the `rewardProgramAuthority` first calls `create_slash_event` to record the slash on-chain, and then `slash` must be called separately for each individual staker's `stake_account_positions`. Because `undelegate` is not blocked between these two steps, a staker who monitors the chain can observe the `create_slash_event` transaction and call `undelegate` before `slash` is executed against their account, escaping the penalty entirely.

---

### Finding Description

The integrity pool program exposes two distinct instructions relevant to slashing:

**Step 1 — `create_slash_event`** (privileged, called by `rewardProgramAuthority`):
Records a `SlashEvent` PDA keyed by `[b"slash_event", publisher, index]` containing `epoch`, `slash_ratio`, and `slash_custody`. It also increments `num_slash_events` in `PoolData`. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

**Step 2 — `slash`** (permissionless `signer`):
Applies the slash to a specific staker's `stake_account_positions` (writable) using the `delegation_record` (writable) which tracks `nextSlashEventIndex`. This must be called once per staker account. [3](#0-2) 

**The gap**: Between Step 1 and Step 2, the `undelegate` instruction remains fully available to any staker. It modifies `stake_account_positions` (writable) and requires only the owner's signature — there is no check that blocks undelegation when a pending slash event exists for the staker's publisher. [4](#0-3) 

The `DelegationRecord` tracks `nextSlashEventIndex` per staker, but this only records which slash events have been *applied* — it does not freeze the staker's position at the epoch of the slash event. Because `slash` operates on the *current* `stake_account_positions` (writable), a staker who has already undelegated has no tokens to slash. [5](#0-4) 

The slashing process necessarily involves multiple separate transactions (one `slash` call per staker across potentially thousands of accounts), creating an unavoidable multi-block window between `create_slash_event` and the completion of all `slash` calls.

---

### Impact Explanation

A staker delegated to a publisher that is about to be slashed can:
1. Observe `create_slash_event` on-chain.
2. Immediately call `undelegate` to remove their entire stake from the publisher pool.
3. The subsequent `slash` call for their account finds zero (or reduced) delegated tokens and applies the slash ratio to nothing.

The staker retains their full PYTH balance while the slashing mechanism — the core security guarantee of OIS — is bypassed. This directly undermines the protocol's ability to penalize delegators who supported a misbehaving publisher. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Solana does not have a traditional public mempool, so classic mempool front-running is not possible. However, `create_slash_event` is an on-chain transaction that is immediately visible once confirmed.
- The `slash` instruction must be called per-staker across a large pool, meaning the window spans multiple blocks — sufficient time for a monitoring staker to react.
- The financial incentive is high: a 5% slash on a large delegation position is a significant loss, making active monitoring worthwhile for large stakers.
- The attack requires no special access — only the staker's own wallet.

Likelihood: **Medium** (requires on-chain monitoring and timely reaction, but is technically straightforward and financially motivated).

---

### Recommendation

Introduce a "slash lock" flag or epoch-based freeze on `undelegate` when a pending slash event exists for the staker's publisher. Specifically:

- In `create_slash_event`, record the current `num_slash_events` index in `PoolData`.
- In `undelegate`, check the staker's `delegation_record.nextSlashEventIndex` against `pool_data.num_slash_events[publisher_index]`. If `nextSlashEventIndex < num_slash_events`, reject the undelegation until `slash` has been applied (i.e., force the staker to process all pending slash events before undelegating).

This is analogous to the recommended mitigation in the reference report: force the state-locking step to occur atomically with the triggering action, rather than relying on correct off-chain ordering.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
1. Staker delegates 1,000,000 PYTH to publisher P.
2. Publisher P causes a data quality incident.
3. Pythian Council calls create_slash_event(publisher=P, index=N, slash_ratio=500) 
   → SlashEvent PDA created with epoch=E, slash_ratio=500 (5%).
4. Staker observes the create_slash_event transaction on-chain.
5. Staker immediately calls undelegate(publisher=P, amount=1_000_000)
   → stake_account_positions updated, delegation removed.
6. Anyone calls slash(publisher=P, index=N, stake_account_positions=Staker)
   → delegation_record.nextSlashEventIndex advances, but staker has 0 tokens 
     delegated to P → 0 PYTH slashed.
7. Staker re-delegates to publisher P (or elsewhere) with full 1,000,000 PYTH intact.

Expected: 50,000 PYTH slashed.
Actual: 0 PYTH slashed.
``` [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7)

  public async unstakeFromPublisher(
    stakeAccountPositions: PublicKey,
    publisher: PublicKey,
    positionState: PositionState.LOCKED | PositionState.LOCKING,
    amount: bigint,
  ) {
    const stakeAccountPositionsData = await this.getStakeAccountPositions(
      stakeAccountPositions,
    );
    const currentEpoch = await getCurrentEpoch(this.connection);

    let remainingAmount = amount;
    const instructionPromises: Promise<TransactionInstruction>[] = [];

    const eligiblePositions = stakeAccountPositionsData.data.positions
      .map((p, i) => ({ index: i, position: p }))
      .reverse()
      .filter(
        ({ position }) =>
          position.targetWithParameters.integrityPool?.publisher !==
            undefined &&
          position.targetWithParameters.integrityPool.publisher.equals(
            publisher,
          ) &&
          positionState === getPositionState(position, currentEpoch),
      );

    for (const { position, index } of eligiblePositions) {
      if (position.amount < remainingAmount) {
        instructionPromises.push(
          this.integrityPoolProgram.methods
            .undelegate(index, convertBigIntToBN(position.amount))
            .accounts({
              publisher,
              stakeAccountPositions,
            })
            .instruction(),
        );
        remainingAmount -= position.amount;
      } else {
        instructionPromises.push(
          this.integrityPoolProgram.methods
            .undelegate(index, convertBigIntToBN(remainingAmount))
            .accounts({
              publisher,
              stakeAccountPositions,
            })
            .instruction(),
        );
        break;
      }
    }

    const instructions = await Promise.all(instructionPromises);
    return sendTransaction(instructions, this.connection, this.wallet);
  }

## Slashing

In the event of a Pyth data inaccuracy that can be verified against independent data references, a portion of the stake can be reduced from the pools aligned with the publishers responsible for the erroneous data.

### Slashing Conditions

A data quality issue happens when the aggregated price from Pyth is deemed inaccurate. Such inaccuracy has to fulfil the following criteria:

- Prevalent prices for the asset(s) in question and related crosses using the most liquid venues at the time of the incident were at least 250 bps away from the price produced by Pyth
- The price deviation have lasted for at least 60 seconds
- Confidence Intervals prints from Pyth do not show an abnormal deviation during the misprint
- The market conditions are normal and the incident is not related to one or many macro events that make the accurate pricing of assets not feasible

### Slashing Calculation and Distribution

If slashing event confirmed, the Pythian Council will process calculation and distribution of the slashed stake according to the following:

- **Stake Slashed**
  - capped at 5% of the total amount staked (including the amount delegated) into pools associated with publishers identified as directly responsible for poor data quality. distribution of the slashed amount is uniform amongst publishers and delegator(s)
  - In the case the total amount staked by the stakers responsible for the data quality issue is nil, no slashing takes place
- **Temporary or Permanent Removal:**
