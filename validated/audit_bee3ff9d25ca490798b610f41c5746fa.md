### Title
Delegator Can `undelegate` After `createSlashEvent` to Escape Slashing — (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

The `undelegate` instruction in the Pyth OIS integrity pool program does not include the `delegationRecord` account and therefore cannot check whether pending (unapplied) slash events exist for the delegator. A delegator who observes a `createSlashEvent` transaction on-chain can immediately call `undelegate` to remove their position before `slash` is called on their account, escaping the penalty entirely. This is the direct analog of H-13: a participant earns rewards while staked but avoids the corresponding loss when a slashing event is triggered.

---

### Finding Description

The OIS slashing lifecycle has two distinct on-chain steps:

1. **`createSlashEvent`** — called by `rewardProgramAuthority`, creates a `SlashEvent` PDA keyed by `[publisher, index]` with `epoch`, `slash_ratio`, and `slash_custody`. This is a single transaction that is immediately visible on-chain.

2. **`slash`** — called per-staker, applies the slash to a specific `stakeAccountPositions`. It reads the `delegationRecord` (which tracks `next_slash_event_index`) to determine which slash events have not yet been applied to that account.

The `DelegationRecord` struct tracks two fields:
- `last_epoch` — last epoch the record was updated
- `next_slash_event_index` — the next slash event index to apply to this delegator [1](#0-0) 

The `slash` instruction correctly includes `delegationRecord` as a writable account so it can advance `next_slash_event_index` and apply the slash ratio to the staker's positions. [2](#0-1) 

However, the `undelegate` instruction's account list does **not** include `delegationRecord`: [3](#0-2) 

Because `delegationRecord` is absent from `undelegate`, the instruction has no way to compare `delegationRecord.next_slash_event_index` against the current number of slash events for the publisher. There is no guard preventing undelegation when unapplied slash events exist.

The `undelegate` instruction is callable by any staker (the `owner` signer is the staker themselves): [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A delegator who monitors the chain for `createSlashEvent` transactions can call `undelegate` in the same or next block, closing their position before `slash` is invoked on their account. When `slash` is subsequently called, the position no longer exists and zero tokens are slashed. The delegator:

- Collected OIS rewards during the epoch they were staked (rewards are distributed at epoch end based on stake during the epoch)
- Bears none of the slashing loss that the protocol intended them to bear

This directly undermines the core security guarantee of OIS: that stakers share the risk of publisher misbehavior proportionally to their delegated stake. The remaining stakers (who did not front-run) absorb a larger effective slash than intended, and the DAO receives less slashed collateral than the `slash_ratio` specifies.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack window opens the moment `createSlashEvent` is confirmed on-chain and closes when `slash` is called for each individual staker's account. On Solana, `slash` must be called separately per staker account — there is no atomic batch slash. A delegator running a monitoring bot can detect `createSlashEvent` in a confirmed block and submit `undelegate` within the same slot or the next slot. The `undelegate` call itself requires no special privilege — only the staker's own signature. Given that slashing events are rare but high-value (up to 5% of pool stake), the economic incentive to front-run is significant for large delegators.

---

### Recommendation

Add `delegationRecord` as a required account to the `undelegate` instruction and enforce that all pending slash events have been applied before undelegation is permitted. Specifically, require:

```
delegationRecord.next_slash_event_index == pool_config.num_slash_events_for_publisher
```

If unapplied slash events exist, the instruction should return `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` (error 6008, which already exists in the program) and require the delegator to call `advance_delegation_record` (which processes pending slashes) before undelegating. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Delegator D stakes 1,000,000 PYTH to publisher P's pool at epoch E. Pool total stake = 10,000,000 PYTH.
2. Publisher P produces a bad price. Pythian Council confirms the incident and calls `createSlashEvent` with `slash_ratio = 500` (5%). `SlashEvent` PDA for `[P, index=0]` is created on-chain.
3. Delegator D's bot detects the `createSlashEvent` transaction in the confirmed block.
4. D immediately calls `undelegate(position_index, 1_000_000)`. This succeeds — `undelegate` does not include `delegationRecord` and cannot check `next_slash_event_index`. D's position is closed.
5. The Pythian Council calls `slash(index=0)` for D's `stakeAccountPositions`. The position no longer exists; 0 PYTH is slashed from D.
6. D retains all 1,000,000 PYTH. The remaining stakers absorb D's share of the slash. D earned rewards during epoch E without bearing the corresponding risk. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)
