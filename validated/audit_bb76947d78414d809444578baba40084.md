### Title
OIS Slash Avoidance via Front-Running `create_slash_event` During Investigation Window — (`governance/pyth_staking_sdk/src/idl/integrity-pool.json`)

---

### Summary

The Oracle Integrity Staking (OIS) slashing mechanism is a two-step process: first `create_slash_event` is called by the `reward_program_authority`, then `slash` is called per-staker. The `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` guard on `undelegate` only activates **after** `create_slash_event` has been called. The Slashing Rulebook explicitly allows up to one full epoch (~7 days) between a misprint incident and the creation of the slash event. During this window, informed delegators can freely call `undelegate`, moving their stake to cooldown before any slash event exists on-chain, potentially escaping the slash entirely. Slow or unaware delegators bear the full slash ratio, creating unequal loss distribution — a direct analog to M-7.

---

### Finding Description

The OIS slashing flow has three on-chain steps:

1. **`create_slash_event`** — called by `reward_program_authority`, creates a `SlashEvent` PDA keyed by `(publisher, index)` storing `epoch`, `slash_ratio`, and `slash_custody`. This increments `pool_data.num_slash_events`.

2. **`slash`** — called per individual staker, applies the slash event at a given `index` to a specific `stake_account_positions`. It updates `delegation_record.next_slash_event_index`.

3. **`undelegate`** — guarded by `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` (error 6008), which checks that `delegation_record.next_slash_event_index == pool_data.num_slash_events`. This prevents undelegating when unapplied slash events exist. [1](#0-0) 

The critical gap: the `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` guard only fires **after** `create_slash_event` has been called. Before that call, `num_slash_events` has not been incremented, so `next_slash_event_index == num_slash_events` trivially, and `undelegate` succeeds unconditionally. [2](#0-1) 

The `DelegationRecord` struct tracks `last_epoch` and `next_slash_event_index`. The `next_slash_event_index` is the sole mechanism preventing slash escape — but it is only meaningful once a slash event exists. [3](#0-2) 

The Slashing Rulebook explicitly states the Pythian Council has until the **end of the epoch following the incident** to conclude its investigation and call `create_slash_event`:

> "The Pythian Council is responsible for analysing and delivering its conclusions within the same epoch when the potential slashing event happened or during the following epoch at the latest." [4](#0-3) 

This creates a publicly observable window of up to **7 days** during which:
- The misprint is visible on-chain and discussed on the public forum
- No `SlashEvent` PDA exists yet
- Any delegator can call `undelegate` without restriction

The `create_slash_event` instruction requires `reward_program_authority` as signer, meaning it is not atomic with the misprint detection — it is a separate, delayed governance action. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Informed delegators (those monitoring the Pyth forum or on-chain data) can call `undelegate` during the investigation window before `create_slash_event` is called. Their positions transition to cooldown state. If the `slash` instruction does not apply to cooldown-state positions, these delegators escape the slash entirely. Remaining delegators — those who are unaware, slow, or simply honest — bear the full `slash_ratio` (up to 5% of their stake). The loss is not distributed equally across all participants who were staked during the misprint epoch, directly violating the stated design intent:

> "distribution of the slashed amount is uniform amongst publishers and delegator(s)" [6](#0-5) 

The security guarantee of OIS — that stakers bear proportional risk for publisher misbehavior — is undermined. Publishers themselves can also self-undelegate during the window, removing their own skin-in-the-game.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The investigation window is **publicly announced** via the Pyth forum, giving all market participants equal visibility.
- The `undelegate` call is a standard, permissionless user action requiring no special access.
- Sophisticated delegators (e.g., large institutional stakers, MEV bots monitoring governance forums) have strong financial incentive to monitor for slash discussions.
- The 7-day window is ample time to act — no block-level speed is required.
- The `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` guard is the only protection, and it is provably inactive before `create_slash_event`. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

1. **Snapshot stake at misprint epoch**: Record the staked amounts per delegator at the epoch of the misprint (e.g., in the `SlashEvent` PDA or a separate snapshot account). Apply the slash ratio against the historical snapshot, not current positions.

2. **Freeze undelegation on slash-eligible pools**: When a misprint is reported (even before formal `create_slash_event`), introduce a preliminary "freeze" instruction callable by `reward_program_authority` that sets a flag on `pool_data` blocking `undelegate` for affected publisher pools.

3. **Reduce investigation window**: Shorten the allowed investigation period to minimize the front-running window, or require `create_slash_event` to be called atomically with the misprint detection.

4. **Apply slash to cooldown positions**: Ensure the `slash` instruction explicitly covers positions in cooldown state, not only `LOCKED` positions, so that undelegating does not constitute an escape path.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**: Publisher P is staked with 10M PYTH self-stake. Delegator D has 1M PYTH delegated to P's pool. The pool has `num_slash_events = 0`. D's `delegation_record.next_slash_event_index = 0`.

**Step 1 — Misprint occurs at epoch T**: Publisher P submits a bad price. The Pyth aggregate deviates >250 bps for >60 seconds. Community members post evidence on the Pyth forum.

**Step 2 — D front-runs (epoch T, same day)**: D observes the forum post. D calls `undelegate` on their 1M PYTH position delegated to P.
- Check: `delegation_record.next_slash_event_index (0) == pool_data.num_slash_events (0)` → passes, no `OutdatedDelegatorAccounting` error.
- D's 1M PYTH moves to cooldown state.

**Step 3 — Investigation proceeds (epoch T to T+1, up to 7 days)**: The Pythian Council reviews evidence. D's stake sits in cooldown.

**Step 4 — `create_slash_event` called at epoch T+1**: `reward_program_authority` calls `create_slash_event` with `index=0`, `slash_ratio=500` (5%). `pool_data.num_slash_events` becomes 1. The `SlashEvent` PDA is created.

**Step 5 — `slash` called on remaining delegators**: The `slash` instruction is called for each staker still delegated to P. Each remaining delegator loses 5% of their stake.

**Step 6 — D's outcome**: If `slash` does not apply to cooldown positions, D's 1M PYTH is untouched. D waits for cooldown to end and withdraws the full 1M PYTH. D bears 0% loss while other delegators bear 5%.

**Result**: Loss distribution is unequal. Informed/fast delegators bear 0% loss; slow/unaware delegators bear the full 5% slash. The total slashed amount is less than intended, weakening the OIS security guarantee. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8) [10](#0-9)

### Slashing Calculation and Distribution

If slashing event confirmed, the Pythian Council will process calculation and distribution of the slashed stake according to the following:

- **Stake Slashed**
  - capped at 5% of the total amount staked (including the amount delegated) into pools associated with publishers identified as directly responsible for poor data quality. distribution of the slashed amount is uniform amongst publishers and delegator(s)
  - In the case the total amount staked by the stakers responsible for the data quality issue is nil, no slashing takes place
